Following Through


It’s a new day and you’ve set a new goal.
Now you can start to follow through with it.
To work towards your goal, it's recommended that you follow some sort of routine or program. Below are some popular programs.

Picking a Program


Starting Strength is a simple, long-term system for getting stronger over time.

Push-Pull-Legs is more of a template than a program, but serves as a great intermediate hypertrophy building routine.

Wendler’s 5/3/1 is a full-body, 3 day a week routine for novice lifters.

These are just some of the many programs out there! If none of these interest you, I’m sure you’ll find one that fits.

Your Own Routine


You don't have to follow a predefined program though!

Personally, I created my own routine and have been following it for close to two years. It's good to experiment slightly and figure out what works best for you.

If you decide to create your own routine, remember to follow the core attributes of your goal!

General Tips


The next few sections are just general tips to keep in mind while at the gym. Even though these just fall under general tips, each one of these is extremely important!

Staying Hydrated


Bring a water bottle to the gym and drink some water between each set or every other set.

Staying hydrated is not just important in general, but is essential for keeping up your performance in the gym. Studies have shown that dehydration can result in up to a 30% decrease in gym performance.

Mind-Muscle Connection


Lifting is more than just a physical activity. There is a level of focus and concentration that is required and, if you want to maximize your gainz, establishing a mind muscle connection is key.

Essentially mind-muscle connection is focusing on the muscle that is being worked while you are performing the lift.

Learn more about mind-muscle connection here.

Stick With It


Starting up is easy, sticking with it is hard.
Make lifting just another part of your day; just like brushing your teeth is a staple part of your day, lifting should be too. This mindset builds discipline and will keep you in the gym making all kinds of gainz.
